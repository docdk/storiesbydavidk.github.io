Welcome to Stories by David Kulikowski

Stories by David Kulikowski is a place where storytelling, family connection, and learning come together. The inspiration came from our family's move from Australia to the USA when Harvey was only 4 months old. We wanted to find a way to capture our feelings and emotions in a way that could help others who have family that they can't easily visit or spend time with, and also help parents and teachers to find ways of engaging their children more deeply in the books they love.

Reading with children is one of the most powerful ways to build confidence, curiosity, and a love of learning. This website has been created to make story time more meaningful, while still keeping it simple, relaxed, and enjoyable for both children and the adults reading with them. Here you will find children’s books alongside thoughtfully designed resources that help extend the experience beyond the story itself. Parents and teachers can easily access activities that encourage conversation, imagination, and deeper thinking, all inspired by the characters and ideas within the books. All activities are designed to feel enjoyable rather than overwhelming, helping children build confidence and motivation as they begin their journey towards becoming lifelong readers and learners.

The free downloadable resources help children explore important early learning concepts in a natural and engaging way, including:

  • understanding fiction and non-fiction
  • recognising rhyme and rhythm
  • learning about Australian animals, plants, and geography
  • exploring sound words and playful language
  • developing curiosity about the world
  • and many more...

For teachers, the resources are designed with links to the Australian Curriculum, while still remaining flexible enough to be adapted for different classrooms, age groups, and learning needs. Although many activities are ideal for children aged 4–10, there are also options for younger learners (ages 2–4), including colouring, memory activities, pattern recognition, and early fine motor skills such as cutting and sorting. Additional teacher resources, including lesson ideas and cross-curriculum links, are also available for those looking for more structured classroom support.

The current collection of resources has been carefully selected to create strong connections with the story, while keeping preparation time low for busy parents and educators. Over time, more resources will continue to be added. Feedback is always welcome. If there is something that would help support learning at home or in the classroom, please feel free to get in touch via the Contact page. The goal of this space is to grow alongside the families and teachers who use it.

Please also explore our 'Idea to Story' section that captures the creative process that went in to creating the books. These show how much the books change thorugh time and how a simple idea that was sketched out became a published book. Hopefully this motivates others to embark on the exciting journey and enjoy the creative process.

Thank you for visiting, and we hope these stories help create meaningful moments of connection, imagination, and learning.

David K.
