From Idea to Story: Harvey's Last Day in Australia

Where the idea began

This story was inspired by our family moving from Australia to Texas when Harvey was just four months old. Preparing to leave familiar places and travel to the other side of the World with a young child made us reflect on how big some changes can feel for families. We spent three years in Texas, travelling to many places that we wouldn't normally visit from Australia, but the long flight from Houston to Perth was something we were too nervous to do with a young family. Our second child Mina was also born in Texas during this time. These reflections helped shape the gentle tone of the book that not matter how far away you may be, your family and friends will always be there for you.

Main Characters

The illustrations started as relatively simple sketches that captured some of my ideas, such as a red cap, sunglasses and backback. This slowly evolved into a more refined sketch, with colours adjusted to match the backgrounds. As the ideas progressed, a more professional digital drawing software was used to begin finalising the illustrations. Minor adjustments continued, such as removing sunglasses and continously assessing the colour palette as the backgrounds changed. As this was the first time writing and illustraitng a book, we made many mistakes and learnt a lot that will help to make the process more efficient next time.

Final book and layout

The final version reflects both the personal journey behind the story and the aim to support children as they experience change. Harvey’s Last Day in Australia is intended to help children feel confident about new experiences, wherever their journey takes them.
