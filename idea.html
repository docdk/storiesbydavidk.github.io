From Idea to Story: Harvey's Last Day in Australia

Where the idea began

This story was inspired by our family moving from Australia to Texas when Harvey was just four months old. Preparing to leave familiar places and travel so far with a young child made me reflect on how big changes can feel for families. I wanted to create a story that helps children see change as something that can feel exciting as well as unfamiliar, and to show that new experiences often bring new opportunities.

Images: Notebook page with first ideas Map showing Australia and Texas Travel photo or suitcase image

A story shaped by family life

Part way through the two year design process, our daughter Mina was born. This led to discussions about whether to include her in the story, and highlighted how quickly family life can change and become more busy. We spent three years in Texas, travelling to many places that we wouldn't have visited otherwise, but the long flight from Houston to Perth was something we were too nervous about doing with two young kids. These reflections helped shape the gentle and reassuring tone of the book that your family is always with you no matter how far apart you might be.

Writing the story

The text was refined to keep the language simple, calm, and easy to read aloud. Each page aims to encourage discussion and help children connect the story to their own experiences of change. The goal was to create a book families can return to when preparing for new beginnings.

Images: Early manuscript draft Printed pages with edits Page layout planning

Main Characters

The illustrations were designed to be simple and uncluttered, with one or two key characters on each spread and minimal background detail. This helps young readers focus on the story while keeping the overall feel calm and engaging.

Final book and layout

The final version reflects both the personal journey behind the story and the aim to support children as they experience change. Harvey’s Last Day in Australia is intended to help children feel confident about new experiences, wherever their journey takes them.
