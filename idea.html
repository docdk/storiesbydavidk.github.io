From Idea to Story: Harvey's Last Day in Australia

Where the idea began

This story was inspired by our family moving from Australia to Texas when Harvey was just four months old. Preparing to leave familiar places and travel to the other side of the World with a young child made us reflect on how big some changes can feel for families. We spent three years in Texas, travelling to many places that we wouldn't normally visit from Australia, but the long flight from Houston to Perth was something we were too nervous to do with a young family. Our second child Mina was also born in Texas during this time. These events helped shape the gentle tone of the book that not matter how far away you may be, your family and friends will always be there for you.

Main Characters

The illustrations started as relatively simple sketches that captured some of my ideas, such as a red cap, sunglasses and backback. This slowly evolved into a more refined sketch, with colours adjusted to match the backgrounds. As the ideas progressed, a more professional digital drawing software was used to begin finalising the illustrations. Minor adjustments continued, such as removing sunglasses and continously assessing the colour palette as the backgrounds changed. As this was the first time writing and illustraitng a book, we made many mistakes and learnt a lot that will help to make the process more efficient next time.

Backgrounds and colour scheme

The initial book ideas were quickly sketched out in a basic software, for the sole purpose of capturing ideas. You can see in the first example there is a yellow squiggly line with a head that is representing a snake. This idea progressed into the more detailed drawing you can now see in the book. The colour scheme also changed, there were some ideas around being more abstract and including more white sapce, before finally deciding on a semi-realistic colour scheme with more minimalistic backgorunds.

Final book and layout

The final version represents a two year journey starting from when the idea was first conceived. What followed was a long process of identifying suitable scenes and animals, aligning the story, creating consistent illustrations, and determining the best approach for publication. Every aspect of the book was scrutinised, from the front cover font, to the text colour, and the best onamotapeas to use within the story. It was a terrific learning, and one that I would recommend to others looking to explore new hobbies and skills.
