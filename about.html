About David Kulikowski

Placeholder introduction: David Kulikowski is a proud father of two young children, Harvey and Mina, and lives in Perth, Australia with his family. His children’s stories are inspired by the small but meaningful moments that shape family life – the adventures, the worries, the laughter, and the imagination that fills childhood. David believes that the best stories help children feel safe, confident, and curious about the world around them. His books are written to be enjoyed together, creating special moments between children and the adults who read with them. Each story is designed not only to spark imagination, but also to support conversations, learning, and connection at home or in the classroom. With guidance from his wife, an experienced teacher, David’s stories and accompanying resources are thoughtfully shaped to align with the Australian Curriculum, making them both engaging and practical for families and educators. His first book, Harvey’s Last Day in Australia, was inspired by his family’s international move when Harvey was just three months old. Like many big life changes, the journey came with a mix of excitement, uncertainty, and emotion. Creating this story became a meaningful way to reflect on those experiences and share them with other families navigating change. Having studied art in his younger years, illustrating this book also allowed David to reconnect with a lifelong passion for creativity. Through his stories, he hopes to create warm, memorable experiences that children will treasure and that adults will feel confident sharing.

Through story, he hopes to encourage curiosity, conversation, creativity, and connection — both at home and in educational settings.
